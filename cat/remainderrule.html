Remainder Rule

Consider the following question (17 * 23) / 12

suppose we have to find the remainder of this expression when divided by 12

we divide each number with 12 and get its remainder

                    
    17 / 12 = remainder 5
    23 / 12 = remainder 11

as it cannot be divided further we multiply the remainder and again try to divide

                
    5 * 11 = 55 
    55 / 12 = remainder 7

7 cannot be divided further hence the remainder of the product is 7 . This is how remainder theorem works

consider the following question (1421 * 1423 * 1425) / 12

lets use the above method to find the remainder 

                    
    1421 / 12 = remainder 5
    1423 / 12 = remainder 7 
    1425 / 12 = remainder 9

we multiple the remainder and try again to divide them with 12

                    
    5 * 7 * 9 = 35 * 9
    35 / 12 = remainder 11 
    remainder 9 * remainder 11 = 99
    99 / 12 = remainder 3

negative remainders

Consider the following question (14 * 15) / 8

lets calculate the remainder in negative values

                    
    14 / 8 = remainder -2 because 8 * 2 = 16 and since 14 is 2 less than 16
    15 /8 = remainder -1 
    multiply the remainder we get the remainder 2

consider the following question 62 * 63 * 64 / 66 using negative remainder

                    
    62 / 66 = -4 remainder
    63 / 66 = -3 remainder
    64 / 66 = -2 remainder

multiply the remainder and try to divide them again

                    
   -4 * -3 * -2 = -24
   -24 / 66 = -24 remainder is not understandable
   8 / 9 = -1 remainder

to get 8 as remainder we have to add 9 and -1 .similarly to achieve the positive remainder number we add 66 with -24 and we achieve 42 as the positive remainder
